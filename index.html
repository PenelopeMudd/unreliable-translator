The work behind the practice.

Longitudinal qualitative research, mixed-methods analysis, and translational study — reading data most people don’t look at, across time periods most people don’t use.

  • Dissertation · UMSL · March 2023
    Design Thinking and Empathy Development in Middle School ELA
    Quasi-experimental mixed methods. 129 seventh-graders, 12 weeks, 5 treatment groups. Finding: RW + DT showed the most promise; empathy development requires sustained, explicit instruction to produce measurable growth.
    Available on request
  • Longitudinal Study · UMSL · June 2026
    Principal Perceptions of First-Year Teacher Preparedness
    Longitudinal convergent mixed-methods analysis. 6 years quantitative, 8 years qualitative. Finding: classroom management is the dominant and persistent gap across both data streams simultaneously.
    Available on request
  • Year 1 CoI Paper · UMSL · June 2026
    Classroom Management Support at Missouri School
    Year 1 RPP site report. Building-level diagnostic using SW-PBS 4:1 benchmark and structured classroom observations across 11 teachers. Finding: management challenges are structural, not individual.
    Available on request

About

Kim Starkey, PhD

For eighteen years I taught middle school English; then I earned a PhD in educational psychology and learned to name what I’d been doing all along — translation. I help people and organizations see what’s already true in their own experience and turn it into something they can use: the patterns hiding in the messy, human, free-text data most systems collect and can’t read.

Post-Doctoral Fellow in Translational Research, UMSL College of Education
PhD, University of Missouri–St. Louis
18 years middle school ELA, Missouri public schools
Student, parent, teacher, researcher — Missouri public education, 53 years
