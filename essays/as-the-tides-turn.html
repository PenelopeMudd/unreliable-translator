As the Tides Turn

quell (n.)

The very top of a wave — especially one ridden, balanced, or claimed by a mermaid.

The quell is the bright, breath-catching moment where the wave is highest, the light is sharpest, and the mermaid feels most her own.

See: feelin’ herself, spark-bits, top of the [thing that’s the best to climb], or a goal for future play

Out in the deep, dark ocean, a figure battles

Alone.

Nearer the shape, nearer the shape, nearer—
Not again! A dinghy flails over another quell

just out of reach

as the Ocean does Her best
to rip the oxygen from this fragile, sinking, silhouette
and replace it with water.

Take that, human, She bellows.
Dare to swim alone, Tiny pitiful air sucker? She trumpets

As she douses the face of the mortal in Her clutches,
all Her glory visible in each punishing crest.

Dare to sail my waves unaided?
You’ve got to be fitted for these waters—
salt-tested, structurally-sound, Little Treader.

While my projects form, I use Chat GPT and cheaters for support.

Off in the distance, like a shiny lavender-hued dolphin with sparkling bits glinting in her hair, a mermaid frolics up one side of the big waves and down its backside.

Feeling proud because, well, she’s a mermaid∼ She flies over and flips her glistening tail to the sun — To the sun and the whole universe, she thinks, Her chaos-gremlin smile causes the hole in her face to appear. Now not only proud, but amused and sure she looks cute…

Ooopsie! She tells herself that she should definitely not like to have that face on regularly. That face, the half smile, the one that forms the one-hole smile, that was the one that got her caught before she could even do it, whatever IT was; she was always struggling with all the its and places, and Creatures.

Well, bothers were not something she liked much and there were many more leaps, splashes, and coddiwomples to do. Although she let the word work itself into the right form in her mouth, as she rolled it around like marbles — Marbles, who would have marbles in their mouth, she scolded herself.

Yes, decidedly round. This word is not pointy.

With new words one simply needed to know their shapes. She liked this one’s shape; a lot — she was always more comfortable with roundy words and roundy creatures. Poky Creatures and Critters are hard to make one very thinky. They’re just so, well, difficult.

Well, no need to spin her brain into sausage. Ha! Sausage was a mushy, blob of a disgusting word. I mean, do you KNOW what’s in a sausage— all of it? All. of. It.

Pleased at both her vocabulary and her generosity, she continued her inner word melody, sigh. She both adored and coveted words: Only daring to think them at first. Let them twirl and plod in her brain safely, practice makes — no, embarrassed to her tail — her tail twitches a bit at the mortification lest she reenact the posse incident of her childhood. How was she supposed to know? She thought it was a Cowboy phrase or something.

Anywho, it was an opportunity to learn, and it did help her develop her Tides. For example: Tide: When acquiring a new rule, she’s sure that she knew it AND heard someone else say it aloud. Words were so fun! This was a new one she collected from a cousin-friend. As she planned her next acrobatic attempt for maximum tail-to-sun exposure.

The sun on her cute little tail
peeks at the sky, greets the universe,
and then splashes back safely to the sea.
Kim Starkey, PhD, unreliable translator
